## How to Set Up Your AI Voice Workflow (Step-by-Step)

### Step 1: Write Your Script
Write for the ear, not the eye. Short sentences. Natural pauses. Contractions. Read it aloud in your head — if it sounds stiff, it'll sound stiff in AI.

### Step 2: Choose Your Voice
Murf AI lets you audition voices by emotion and use case. Pick a voice that matches your content's tone — authoritative for tutorials, warm for storytelling, energetic for listicles.

### Step 3: Add Emphasis and Pauses
This is where most beginners fail. AI voices default to flat delivery. In Murf, manually add:
- **Emphasis** on key words
- **Pauses** after punchlines and before transitions
- **Speed variations** — slow down for important points, speed up for examples

### Step 4: Layer in Background Music
Both Murf and Speechify include royalty-free music libraries. A subtle background track masks any remaining AI artifacts and boosts perceived production value.

### Step 5: Export and Sync
Export as MP3 or WAV, drop into your video editor, and align with your visuals. Murf's built-in video sync eliminates this step entirely.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Using the default voice without customization** — spend 10 minutes tuning emphasis and pauses. It's the difference between "obviously AI" and "wait, that's AI?"
2. **Ignoring platform audio specs** — YouTube wants 48 kHz; TikTok needs specific loudness levels. Export correctly.
3. **Skipping background music** — even quiet ambient music masks subtle AI artifacts.
4. **Not A/B testing voices** — post the same content with two different voices and check retention graphs. Platform algorithms reward higher watch time.
