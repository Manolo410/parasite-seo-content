## Method 2: Rewrite Sentence-Level Syntax (Not Just Words)

**The #1 mistake students make:** Swapping synonyms ("utilize" → "use," "demonstrate" → "show") and thinking they've beaten detection.

**What detectors actually measure:** The **ratio of complex to simple sentence constructions** across your document. AI maintains a steady, predictable ratio. Human writing varies wildly.

**Practical fix:**
- Alternate between passive and active voice within each section
- After every 3-4 complex sentences, insert a short, declarative statement
- Break up strings of subordinate clauses — they're an AI signature

> **Core principle:** Syntax is the signal; vocabulary is a distraction. Fix your sentence structure, not just your word choices.

---

## Method 3: Break Uniform Paragraph Structure

AI-generated text produces suspiciously uniform paragraphs — similar length, identical internal structure (topic → evidence → transition). This regularity is a major detection signal.

**How to fix it:**
- Insert at least **two short paragraphs (1-2 sentences)** per major section
- Vary paragraph length dramatically — some 8 sentences, some 2
- Split one long paragraph mid-argument if logically justifiable

These changes alter distributional features that AI detectors directly measure. It's the cheapest, fastest improvement you can make.

## Method 4: Integrate Real Source Language and Direct Quotations

**Why this works brilliantly:** Direct quotations from published sources are, by definition, not AI-generated. Every properly cited quote pulls your overall detection score toward "human."

**Placement strategy:**
- Front-load quotes in sections that feel AI-heavy
- Let source language carry the first 2-3 paragraphs of your literature review
- When paraphrasing, preserve the **specific logical move** of the original source — not just the conclusion. This forces phrasing that reflects an actual text you read rather than averaged training data.

**Bonus:** You should be citing sources anyway. This method leverages an academic requirement that's already expected of you.

## Method 5: Vary Perplexity Deliberately

**Perplexity** measures how predictable each word is given the preceding text. AI text has **low perplexity** (chooses statistically probable words). Human academic writing has **higher perplexity** (uses precise, uncommon disciplinary terms).

**Two concrete actions:**

1. **Use exact disciplinary vocabulary:** Write "epistemological framework" not "knowledge system." Write "homologous structures" not "similar parts." This precision makes your word choices less predictable — a strong human signal.

2. **Insert occasional plain-language sentences:** After a dense technical paragraph, drop: *"Put simply: the data doesn't support this conclusion."* This register mixing between technical and plain language is distinctly human.

## Method 6: Use Field-Specific Vocabulary Naturally

AI models average across disciplines. This causes **cross-disciplinary vocabulary bleed** — a sociology paper using psychology jargon, or a biology paper borrowing chemistry terminology. Human experts stick to their field's specific terms.

**Audit your draft:**
- Check your introduction and literature review — these are the most visible, most analyzed sections
- Replace borrowed terms with your field's preferred vocabulary
- If you're in political science, use "hegemony" and "discourse" — not the generic alternatives AI favors

**Why this matters:** Even Purdue University acknowledges that AI detectors have significant false-positive rates. Using your field's precise language makes it harder for detectors to confuse your work with generic AI output.

## Method 7: Add Personal Academic Voice

**The ultimate anti-detection strategy:** Inject something the AI couldn't have written.

- Reference a specific class discussion, a professor's unique argument, or a campus-specific resource
- Include a personal observation about the research process ("When I first encountered Rawls' veil of ignorance concept, it reminded me of...")
- Connect two sources in a way that reflects YOUR intellectual journey through the material

No AI detector can flag content that's genuinely, uniquely yours. This is your strongest defense — and it makes your paper better in the process.
